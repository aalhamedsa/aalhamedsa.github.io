Integrated Communication and Sensing Systems Group

Research

Phased Array Systems for 5G/6G Applications

Our research focuses on the development of advanced phased-array systems for 5G and 6G communication applications. We investigate novel architectures and design techniques to enhance performance, reduce power consumption, and improve the overall efficiency of these systems. Our work spans from the individual component level to system-level optimization, enabling us to deliver state-of-the-art solutions for future wireless communication networks.

Secure IoT and RFID Hardware

Security and privacy are critical aspects of the rapidly growing Internet of Things (IoT) and Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) ecosystems. Our group is researching innovative hardware solutions to enhance the security and robustness of these systems. We are developing new cryptographic primitives, secure communication protocols, and tamper-resistant hardware designs to protect sensitive data and ensure the integrity of IoT and RFID devices.

Advanced AI Hardware Accelerators Design

Artificial intelligence (AI) has emerged as a powerful tool in various fields, but its efficient deployment often requires specialized hardware accelerators. Our research group focuses on the design and development of advanced AI hardware accelerators that can significantly enhance the performance and energy efficiency of AI applications in areas such as natural language processing, computer vision, and machine learning.

Quantum Monolithic Integrated Systems

Quantum computing has the potential to revolutionize various fields, and the development of monolithic integrated systems is a key aspect of this technology. Our research group focuses on designing and implementing quantum monolithic integrated systems that can leverage the unique properties of quantum mechanics to achieve exponential improvements in processing power, communication speeds, and energy efficiency.
